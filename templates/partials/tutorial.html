Feature Introduction

  • Main Explorer: The core 3D interactive scene. You can click, drag, and scroll to explore star systems and make AI predictions on planets.
  • Model Evaluation: Click the "Model Evaluation" button at the top to view performance reports for four pre-trained AI models, including accuracy and confusion matrices.
  • Search System: Quickly search for and jump to a target system from thousands of known star systems by name or number.
  • Analysis Dashboard: Access this page from the navigation bar after selecting a planet. It provides detailed planetary data, where you can adjust parameters and observe real-time changes in AI confidence.
  • Kepler View: Simulates the perspective of the Kepler Space Telescope, observing dips in brightness (transits) as planets pass in front of their host stars, displayed as light curve graphs.
  • Model Training: Upload your own exoplanet candidate data (or use sample files), select an AI model, and adjust hyperparameters to train a temporary, custom model online.

Transit Method

The transit method is one of the most successful and widely used techniques for detecting exoplanets. When a planet passes in front of its host star, it blocks a small portion of the starlight, causing a periodic and slight dip in brightness. By precisely measuring these light curves, scientists can infer the existence of planets, their size, orbital period, and even some atmospheric characteristics. The depth of the brightness dip depends on the relative size of the planet compared to its star, while the frequency of transits reflects the planet's orbital period.
